An FBAR (Foreign Bank Account Report) is sent directly to the Department of Treasury to report foreign bank account balances. If you had more than $10,000 USD worth of foreign currency in one account, or a total of $10,000 USD spread across multiple foreign bank accounts, you need to file an FBAR.

Taxes are due in mid-April each year, but filers who are outside of the US and its territories on April 15th get an automatic extension until June 15. It is generally understood that expat taxes are due in June — however, if you owe taxes, those payments are due in April, and interest will start to accrue after the standard April deadline.
